High-Yield Bond Fund

Definition: 
Invests in lower-rated corporate bonds (BB or lower) offering higher yields (6-12%) to compensate for higher default risk.

Structure:
•  Investors buy shares in fund
•  Fund invests in portfolio of high-yield bonds
•  Income generated from bond interest payments

Objective: 
Attract investors seeking higher income streams, willing to accept higher risk.

Fixed Income Fund

Definition: 
Invests in various debt securities (bonds, treasury notes) with fixed interest rates and maturity dates.

Structure:
•  Similar to high-yield bond fund
•  Invests in higher-rated bonds (A or higher), government securities, and mortgage-backed securities

Objective: 
Preserve capital while generating stable income streams with lower risk.

Similarities:
1.  Both invest in debt securities
2.  Both offer regular income streams
3.  Both have various maturity dates and credit ratings

Differences:
1.  Credit Rating: High-yield funds invest in lower-rated bonds, fixed income funds in higher-rated bonds.
2.  Yield: High-yield funds offer higher yields, fixed income funds offer lower yields.
3.  Risk: High-yield funds have higher default risk, fixed income funds have lower default risk.
